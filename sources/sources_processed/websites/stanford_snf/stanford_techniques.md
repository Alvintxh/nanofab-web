# Stanford Nanofabrication Facility - Techniques and Projects Guide

## Overview

The Techniques section serves as a comprehensive resource for nanofabrication processing methods and practical implementation guidance at Stanford's facility.

## Main Content Areas

### Processing Techniques
The facility organizes information by processing method type, providing a starting point for researchers familiar with general categories of fabrication work.

### Projects and Processes
The guide collects "practical, detailed, how-to information that will hopefully help you design and troubleshoot your process." This includes:

- **Fab Project Courses (E241 & EE412)**: Project-based courses focused on developing and characterizing processing methods
- **Community Service Projects**: Lab members can propose initiatives benefiting the broader community
- **nano@stanford Fellowship Projects**: Research initiatives from fellowship recipients

### Nano Nuggets
Described as "bite-size pieces of processing knowledge," these resources include:
- Standard Operating Procedures (SOPs) for basic processes
- Practical insights from researchers
- Troubleshooting solutions

### Additional Resources
- Runsheets with common procedures
- Process documentation
- Equipment-specific guidance organized by technique (CVD, dry etch, photolithography, metallization, etc.)

## Support Structure
The facility provides training videos, safety protocols, materials databases, and staff liaisons organized by processing specialty to support user success.
