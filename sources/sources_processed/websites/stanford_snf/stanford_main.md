# Stanford Nanofabrication Facility Lab User Guide

## Overview

The SNF Lab User Guide serves as a community resource for nanofabrication techniques and laboratory operations. The guide is "a work-in-progress" with continuous improvements aimed at providing "a community web resource for fabrication in general as well as details about working in our lab."

## Main Sections

### Guide Navigation
- **Guide Home Overview** - Portal to all resources
- **Lab Spaces** - Physical facility descriptions
- **Visit** - Information on lab attire and access
- **How to Join** - Membership procedures
- **Contacts and Emergency** - Staff and safety contacts
- **Rates** - Facility pricing (external link)
- **Storage** - Material and equipment storage
- **History** - Laboratory background

### Key Resource Categories

**Equipment & Techniques**
- Individual equipment pages with operational details
- Processing technique classifications (Photolithography, CVD, Dry Etch, Wet Chemical Processing, Metallization, Doping, etc.)
- Material-based tool searches
- Project-linked equipment references

**Materials & Chemicals**
- Comprehensive chemicals list (acids, bases, developers, etchants, resists, solvents)
- Substrate types and sizes
- Gases inventory
- Cleanliness group classifications ("All," "Clean," "Clean-Ge," "Flexible")

**Training & Safety**
- Online training courses
- Training videos
- Equipment-specific shadowing forms
- Safety data sheets (SDS)
- Gowning procedures for Cleanroom L107
- Safety training requirements

**Project Categories**
- Fab Project Courses (E241 & EE412)
- Device Process Courses (EE410 & EE312)
- Community Service Projects
- nano@stanford Fellowship Projects
- Runsheets and process documentation

## Notable Features

**Integrated Systems**
The guide emphasizes cross-linking between resources: "links from equipment pages to projects that have been done using that equipment (and vice versa)" enable users to explore multiple access points based on their needs.

**Process & Materials Requests (PROM)**
Structured system for introducing new processes or materials, including committee review, TMAH protocols, and archived submissions.

**External Integration**
- NEMO laboratory management system access
- NEMO User Guide documentation
- Tool monitoring capabilities
- Safety training portal (allensafety)
