## Announcements

### DREAMS Hub Awarded 2 Projects in GaN and 5G/6G Technologies
CA DREAMS Hub received $31.9 million in Microelectronics Commons funding:
- $16.2M for advanced gallium nitride semiconductor technologies
- $15.7M for 5G/6G millimeter-wave phased-array prototypes

Partners include USC, Northrop Grumman, Teledyne, HRL Laboratories, UCLA, and UC Santa Barbara.

### NSF-ATE Award for SBCC and UCSB
The project "Expansion of CCPRIME" aims to build a semiconductor pathway at Santa Barbara City College using UCSB cleanrooms. This is part of an Intel-NSF partnership supporting six projects.

### CHIPS Act Award Announced
UCSB NanoFab received recognition as part of the California DREAMS Hub led by USC through the CHIPS & Science Act initiative.

### RIE#3 Removed
RIE#3 has been removed from the Nanofab and moved to the Teaching Cleanroom. User processes transferred to the Fluorine ICP Etcher.

### NanoFab Staff Awarded Goleta's Innovator of the Year 2023
Staff member Demis D. John received the City of Goleta's award for 2023 recognizing the facility's community impact.

## General Information

**Key Responsibilities:**
- NanoFab Users are responsible for understanding their chemistry, risks and usage of various chemicals.
- Review MSDS sheets for all chemicals used
- Users may contribute processing data to Wiki pages

**Supervisor Responsibilities:**
- Supervisors ensure trainees have appropriate knowledge and training
- Ensure regulatory compliance including OSHA Chemical Hygiene Plans
- Wiki information serves as supporting material, not replacement

**Contact:** For questions about the wiki, contact the NanoFab IT Team (nanofab-it@ece.ucsb.edu)
