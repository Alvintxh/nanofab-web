# Vacuum Deposition Recipes - UCSB Nanofab Wiki

## Overview

This page serves as a comprehensive resource for vacuum deposition processes and materials available at the UCSB Nanofab facility. It provides process maturity rankings and links to detailed recipe documentation.

## Process Control Data

Detailed calibration information is maintained on a separate page, including deposition rates, refractive indices, and stress measurements tracked over time for frequently-used equipment and films.

## Process Maturity Ranking System

The facility uses a six-tier ranking system:

- **R6**: Most mature process with regular calibrations recorded on SPC charts
- **R5**: Process with documented procedure, regular data (>=4x yearly), and in-situ control available
- **R4**: Documented procedure with regular data but no in-situ control
- **R3**: Documented procedure with available data
- **R2**: Documented procedure
- **R1**: Least mature - process executed at least once
- **A**: Allowed and materials available but never performed

## Available Deposition Technologies

The facility maintains recipes for six primary deposition techniques:

1. **E-Beam Evaporation** (4 systems: Sharon, Custom, Temescal, CHA)
2. **Sputtering** (5 systems: AJA ATC 2000-F, AJA ATC 2200-V variants, Ion Beam Deposition)
3. **Thermal Evaporation** (2 systems)
4. **PECVD** (3 systems: PlasmaTherm, Advanced Vacuum, Unaxis VLR)
5. **Atomic Layer Deposition** (Oxford FlexAL)
6. **Molecular Vapor Deposition**

## Materials Coverage

The comprehensive materials table includes recipes for over 60 materials, ranging from pure metals (Au, Ag, Ti, Ni, Cr) to oxides (SiO2, Al2O3, HfO2) and nitrides (SiN, TiN, AlN). Maturity levels vary significantly by material and deposition method.
