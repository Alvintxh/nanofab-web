# Plasma Application in Atomic Layer Etching
- **Authors:** Andreas Fischer, Thorsten Lill
- **Journal:** Physics of Plasmas (AIP)
- **Year:** 2023
- **DOI:** 10.1063/5.0145604
- **URL:** https://pubs.aip.org/aip/pop/article/30/8/080601/2906841/Plasma-application-in-atomic-layer-etching

## Abstract
This perspective article discusses the important role of plasma in atomic layer etching (ALE), including both plasma-enhanced and thermal ALE approaches for nanometer-scale device manufacturing. The article compares the advantages as well as challenges of ALE in contrast to classic reactive ion etching (RIE). It includes a comprehensive tally of known plasma-based ALE processes and describes novel thermal ALE processes based on the ligand addition mechanism. The authors discuss how plasma ALE achieves directional etching through sequential modification and removal steps, where a plasma is used in at least one step to provide either surface modification (e.g., halogenation) or the energetic species needed for removal. The review highlights ALE's superior selectivity, damage control, and atomic-level precision compared to continuous plasma etching.

## Key Points
- Comprehensive perspective on the role of plasma in ALE processes
- Compares plasma ALE with conventional reactive ion etching (RIE)
- Catalogs known plasma-based ALE processes for various materials
- Describes novel ligand-addition mechanism for thermal ALE
- Discusses trade-offs: throughput vs. precision, directionality vs. damage
