# 30 Years of Nanoimprint: Development, Momentum and Prospects
- **Authors:** Wei-Kuan Lin, L. Jay Guo
- **Journal:** Opto-Electronic Technology
- **Year:** 2025
- **DOI:** 10.29026/oet.2025.250001
- **URL:** https://www.oejournal.org/oet/article/doi/10.29026/oet.2025.250001

## Abstract
This article marks the 30th anniversary of nanoimprint lithography (NIL), which emerged in 1995 as a primary alternative to extreme ultraviolet lithography for deep-nanoscale silicon electronics. NIL has achieved widespread recognition among semiconductor manufacturers for its manufacturing quality and is actively being evaluated for producing advanced semiconductor devices. The technology's applications extend beyond silicon chip fabrication to wafer-scale uses. With its high throughput and 3D patterning capabilities, NIL is becoming a key technology for fabricating emerging devices, such as flat optics and augmented reality glasses. The review synthesizes key developments and applications over three decades, with particular focus on recent industry advances in nano-silicon device manufacturing and nanophotonics applications.

## Key Points
- Comprehensive 30-year retrospective of nanoimprint lithography (1995-2025)
- NIL demonstrated resolution better than 10 nm, competitive with EUV for certain applications
- Canon's J-FIL technology enables patterning at 14 nm minimum linewidth (5 nm node equivalent)
- Growing applications in flat optics, AR glasses, and nanophotonics beyond traditional CMOS
- Authored by L. Jay Guo, a pioneer in nanoimprint and nanophotonics research
