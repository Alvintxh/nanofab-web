# Area-Selective Atomic Layer Deposition on 2D Monolayer Lateral Superlattices
- **Authors:** (Nature Communications research article authors)
- **Journal:** Nature Communications
- **Year:** 2024
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-024-46293-w
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s41467-024-46293-w

## Abstract
This study reports a superlattice-based area-selective ALD (SAS-ALD) process using a two-dimensional MoS2-MoSe2 lateral superlattice as a pre-defining template, achieving a minimum half pitch size at a sub-10 nm scale. Area-selective deposition (ASD) utilizes either intrinsic differences in substrate surface reactivity or chemically engineered surface inhibitors to achieve self-aligned, bottom-up patterning with high selectivity. The SAS-ALD process demonstrates that intrinsic chemical differences between 2D materials in lateral heterostructures can enable highly selective deposition without the need for external inhibitor molecules, opening pathways for next-generation nanoelectronic device fabrication with sub-10 nm feature sizes.

## Key Points
- Demonstrates area-selective ALD on 2D material lateral superlattices at sub-10 nm scale
- Exploits intrinsic chemical selectivity between MoS2 and MoSe2 — no inhibitor molecules needed
- Achieves bottom-up, self-aligned patterning compatible with next-generation nanoelectronics
- Published in Nature Communications (high-impact, open access)
- Bridges ALD technology with 2D materials research
