# Cu-Cu Hybrid Bonding Technology: From Physical Mechanisms to System Integration for 3D ICs
- **Authors:** (Moore and More journal article — Springer Nature)
- **Journal:** Moore and More (Springer Nature)
- **Year:** 2025
- **DOI:** 10.1007/s44275-025-00036-1
- **URL:** https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s44275-025-00036-1

## Abstract
Cu-Cu hybrid bonding has emerged as an innovative solution to overcome the limitations of traditional packaging, leveraging its outstanding benefits in sub-micron interconnection and efficient thermal management. This review systematically explores the physical mechanisms, integration technologies, and key process breakthroughs of Cu-Cu hybrid bonding for 3D IC applications. The article covers the evolution from micro-bump to hybrid bonding interconnects, discussing surface preparation, CMP requirements, bonding mechanisms (Cu-Cu and oxide-oxide bonding), and alignment precision. Achieving 2 um pitch die-to-wafer and 2.5 um pitch wafer-to-wafer hybrid bonding demonstrates the technology's capability to meet future thermal and electrical requirements for high-power logic/memory stacking applications. The review discusses commercial implementations in CMOS image sensors, high-bandwidth memory (HBM), and 3D NAND, as well as the roadmap toward sub-micron pitch interconnects.

## Key Points
- Comprehensive review of Cu-Cu hybrid bonding mechanisms and integration for 3D ICs
- Covers sub-micron pitch interconnects (down to 2 um pitch demonstrated)
- Discusses surface preparation, CMP, bonding physics, and alignment requirements
- Reviews commercial adoption: CMOS image sensors, HBM, 3D NAND
- Roadmap toward sub-1 um pitch for future chiplet architectures
