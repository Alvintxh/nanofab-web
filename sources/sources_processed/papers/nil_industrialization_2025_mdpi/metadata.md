# Review of Industrialization Development of Nanoimprint Lithography Technology
- **Authors:** (MDPI Metrology journal article)
- **Journal:** Metrology (MDPI)
- **Year:** 2024
- **DOI:** 10.3390/metrology4010010
- **URL:** https://www.mdpi.com/2674-0729/4/1/10

## Abstract
This review examines the industrialization development of nanoimprint lithography (NIL) technology, covering its significant advantages including low cost, high resolution, and elimination of development steps. NIL is not affected by standing wave effects that limit optical lithography, making it a promising technology for semiconductors, photovoltaics, and LEDs. The review covers major NIL variants including jet-and-flash imprint lithography (J-FIL), roll-to-roll (R2R) NIL, and thermal NIL, with emphasis on their industrial adoption status. Key application areas discussed include patterned sapphire substrates (PSSs), LED chips, photovoltaic cells, and DRAM/logic semiconductor devices. The article addresses remaining challenges in film characteristics and material selection, overlay accuracy, template lifetime, and defect control that must be resolved for broader industrial adoption.

## Key Points
- Reviews NIL industrialization status across semiconductors, photovoltaics, and LEDs
- Covers J-FIL (Canon), R2R NIL, and thermal NIL variants for different applications
- NIL demonstrated at 14 nm linewidth (5 nm node), with roadmap to 10 nm (2 nm node)
- Canon, Dai Nippon Printing, and KIOXIA recognized for NIL manufacturing achievements
- Identifies key challenges: overlay accuracy, template lifetime, defect control, throughput

## Relevance to Nanofab Book
This review provides the industrial adoption perspective for NIL, complementing the process-focused coverage in textbooks with real-world manufacturing status and challenges across multiple application domains.
