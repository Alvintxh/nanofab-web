# Low-Cost Energy-Efficient EUV Lithography for Advanced Semiconductor Manufacturing
- **Authors:** (Nature Reviews Electrical Engineering article)
- **Journal:** Nature Reviews Electrical Engineering
- **Year:** 2024
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s44287-024-00129-3
- **URL:** https://www.nature.com/articles/s44287-024-00129-3

## Abstract
This review article examines the economic and energy challenges of EUV lithography deployment for advanced semiconductor manufacturing. As EUV has become indispensable for patterning at the 7 nm node and beyond, the cost per wafer exposure and energy consumption of EUV systems have become critical concerns. Each ASML EUV scanner costs over $150 million (High-NA exceeds $350 million), and a single tool consumes approximately 1 MW of power during operation. The article discusses strategies for reducing cost and energy consumption, including higher source power (reducing exposure time), improved resist sensitivity (lower dose-to-print), and computational lithography optimizations. The review also addresses multi-patterning trade-offs: while EUV reduces the number of patterning steps compared to DUV multi-patterning, High-NA EUV may still require double patterning for the tightest pitches. The economic analysis considers total cost of ownership, including mask costs, tool utilization, and yield impact.

## Key Points
- EUV tools cost $150M+ (High-NA >$350M) and consume ~1 MW during operation
- Strategies for cost reduction: higher source power, better resist sensitivity, computational lithography
- Multi-patterning trade-offs between EUV and DUV approaches analyzed
- Energy efficiency increasingly important as fabs scale EUV tool counts
- Total cost of ownership framework including mask, tool, and yield considerations

## Relevance to Nanofab Book
This article provides essential economic context for EUV lithography adoption, complementing the technical coverage with practical cost and energy considerations that are critical for understanding semiconductor manufacturing decisions.
