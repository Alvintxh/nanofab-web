# Roadmap for Focused Ion Beam Technologies
- **Authors:** Katja Höflich et al. (multi-author roadmap, >30 contributing authors)
- **Journal:** Applied Physics Reviews (AIP)
- **Year:** 2023
- **DOI:** 10.1063/5.0162597
- **URL:** https://pubs.aip.org/aip/apr/article/10/4/041311/2931107/Roadmap-for-focused-ion-beam-technologies

## Abstract
This roadmap article provides a comprehensive overview of focused ion beam (FIB) instrumentation, theory, techniques, and applications. FIB instruments use a range of ion species and are applied broadly in materials science, physics, chemistry, biology, medicine, and archaeology. The roadmap covers developments of new ion sources including xenon plasma FIB (offering material removal rates approximately 100 times faster than traditional Ga+ source FIB) and helium ion microscopy (HIM) enabling fabrication of sub-10 nm scale structures. The article identifies future pathways for ion source and instrumentation development, discusses advances in gas-assisted FIB processing, FIB-induced deposition (FIBID) for 3D nanoprinting, and cryo-FIB for biological sample preparation. The roadmap format brings together contributions from leading FIB researchers worldwide to map the current state and future directions of the field.

## Key Points
- Comprehensive roadmap by 30+ leading FIB researchers worldwide
- Covers Ga+ LMIS, He+ GFIS, Ne+ GFIS, and Xe+ plasma FIB sources
- Plasma FIB offers ~100x faster material removal than traditional Ga+ FIB
- Helium ion microscopy enables sub-10 nm fabrication and imaging
- Discusses FIBID 3D nanoprinting, cryo-FIB, and gas-assisted processing
