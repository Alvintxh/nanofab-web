# Breakthrough in Semiconductor Patterning: New Block Copolymer Achieves 7.6 nm Line Width
- **Authors:** Researchers from Tokyo Institute of Technology (Tokyo Tech) and Tokyo Ohka Kogyo (TOK)
- **Journal:** (Published 2024, reported via ScienceDaily)
- **Year:** 2024
- **DOI:** 10.1038/s41467-024-49839-0
- **URL:** https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11227500/

## Abstract
Researchers from Tokyo Institute of Technology and Tokyo Ohka Kogyo developed a novel block copolymer (BCP) for directed self-assembly (DSA) that achieves a record 7.6 nm line width for semiconductor patterning. The new BCP, designated PS-b-PGFM, was created from polystyrene-block-poly(methyl methacrylate) (PS-b-PMMA) with poly(glycidyl methacrylate) (PGMA) introduced into the PS-b-PMMA backbone. The PGMA was then modified with different thiols to refine the repulsive interactions (Flory-Huggins chi parameter) between the blocks. This approach enables sub-10 nm patterning through DSA, pushing beyond the resolution limits of conventional PS-b-PMMA BCPs (which are limited to ~11 nm half-pitch). The achievement represents a significant step toward enabling DSA for next-generation semiconductor manufacturing at the 2 nm node and beyond.

## Key Points
- Achieves 7.6 nm line width — a record for BCP-based DSA patterning
- Novel PS-b-PGFM copolymer with tunable chi parameter via thiol modification
- Overcomes the ~11 nm resolution limit of conventional PS-b-PMMA BCPs
- Joint industry-academia work (Tokyo Tech + TOK) demonstrating practical applicability
- Targets semiconductor patterning at 2 nm node and beyond

### Introduction

As semiconductor device performance advances rapidly, the need for increasingly finer circuit patterns becomes critical. By 2037, the required minimum half-pitch for 0.5 nm logic nodes will be as small as 8 nm. The integration of directed self-assembly (DSA) of block copolymers (BCPs) with EUV lithography emerges as a promising next-generation lithography candidate.

PS-b-PMMA has received the most attention for DSA due to: (1) nearly identical surface free energies at annealing temperature; (2) random copolymers available for interfacial control; (3) selective PMMA removal for easy pattern transfer. However, the smallest useful features in PS-b-PMMA are ~11 nm due to thermodynamic block mixing with shorter chains.

This study introduces 2,2,2-trifluoroethyl groups into PMMA segments of PS-b-PMMA, creating PS-b-PGFM with higher chi values while maintaining similar surface and interfacial properties. This enables sub-10 nm features without requiring top-coat layers or solvent-vapor annealing.

### Results

**Synthesis:** PS-b-PGMs synthesized by sequential living anionic polymerization, then functionalized with thiols via LiOH-catalyzed epoxide-ring-opening chemistry. Quantitative conversion achieved for most polymers.

**Bulk morphologies:** PS-b-PGFM19-23 showed d-spacing of 19.5 nm (larger than precursor 16.5 nm), indicating higher inter-segment repulsion. PS-b-PGFM10-22 formed lamellar structure with d-spacing of 12.4 nm, while its precursor PS-b-PGM10-22 was disordered, confirming the trifluoroethyl group effectively increases chi.

**Chi values determined:** PS-b-PGFM with 10 mol% PGMAF exhibited chi_eff = 0.110 (3.5x that of PS-b-PMMA at 0.031). With 22 mol%: chi_eff = 0.133. With 30 mol%: chi_eff = 0.142.

**Thin films:** PS-b-PGFM formed perpendicular lamellar structures with L0 under 20 nm after simple thermal annealing. Minimum L0 of 12.3 nm achieved (vs. 17.5 nm minimum for PS-b-PMMA). Cross-sectional SEM confirmed perpendicular lamellae extending from surface to substrate.

**DSA results:** 5x and 6x density multiplications achieved on PS chemical patterns with 84-90 nm pitch. The smallest feature was 7.6 nm -- sub-10 nm line patterns obtained in a highly reliable and reproducible manner with PGMA contents less than 11 mol%.

### Discussion

PS-b-PGFM polymers with <23 mol% PGMA were compatible with DSA process. Introducing 10-23 mol% trifluoroethyl groups increased chi_eff without significantly changing the surface free energy of the PMMA segment. PS-b-PGFM BCPs are promising templates for BCP lithography because they produce fine patterns in a similar DSA process to PS-b-PMMA and have potential to outperform PS-b-PMMA.

### Methods

Synthesis via sequential living anionic polymerization of styrene and GMA/MMA mixture, initiated by sec-BuLi with LiCl/DPE in THF at -78 C. Post-functionalization with 2,2,2-trifluoroethanethiol via thiol-epoxy reaction. Characterization by NMR, SEC, SAXS, TEM, AFM, SEM. DSA performed on SiN/X-PS patterned substrates from imec, with PS-r-PMMA-r-PHEMA neutral layers, annealed at 230-240 C for 5 min.
