# Piezoelectric Thin Films and Their Applications in MEMS: A Review

## Abstract
This comprehensive review covers recent developments in piezoelectric thin films and their applications in microelectromechanical systems (MEMS). The article examines piezoelectric effects, film preparation methods, film properties, and device performance indicators for various piezoelectric MEMS devices. Materials covered include lead zirconate titanate (PZT), aluminum nitride (AlN), scandium-doped aluminum nitride (AlScN), zinc oxide (ZnO), and emerging lead-free piezoelectric materials such as potassium sodium niobate (KNN). The review discusses fabrication techniques including sol-gel processing, sputtering, and pulsed laser deposition, with emphasis on CMOS-compatible processes. Applications span gyroscopes, inkjet print heads, bulk acoustic wave (BAW) filters, autofocus actuators, piezoelectric micromachined ultrasonic transducers (PMUTs), and energy harvesters. The transition from bulk-type to thin-film-type piezoelectric devices driven by microfabrication advances and market demands is highlighted.

## Key Points
- Reviews PZT, AlN, AlScN, ZnO, and KNN piezoelectric thin films for MEMS
- Covers fabrication methods: sol-gel, sputtering, PLD, with CMOS compatibility focus
- Applications: BAW filters, PMUTs, gyroscopes, energy harvesters, inkjet heads
- ScAlN emerging as high-performance lead-free alternative to PZT
- KNN demonstrated as mass-producible piezoelectric (8-inch wafers by Shibata et al. 2022)
