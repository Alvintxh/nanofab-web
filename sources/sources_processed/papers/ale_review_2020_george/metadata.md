# Mechanisms of Thermal Atomic Layer Etching
- **Authors:** Steven M. George, Younghee Lee
- **Journal:** Accounts of Chemical Research (ACS)
- **Year:** 2020
- **DOI:** 10.1021/acs.accounts.0c00084
- **URL:** https://pubs.acs.org/doi/10.1021/acs.accounts.0c00084

## Abstract
Atomic layer control of semiconductor processing is needed as critical dimensions are progressively reduced below the 10 nm scale. Atomic layer deposition (ALD) methods can produce conformal thin film growth on high aspect ratio features. Atomic layer etching (ALE) techniques utilizing sequential, self-limiting reactions based on surface modification and volatile release are also required. Thermal ALE has been developed for metal oxides (Al2O3, HfO2, ZrO2, ZnO, WO3, SiO2), nitrides (SiN, TiN, AlN), elements (Ni, Co, Mo, Si, W), and sulfides (ZnS). The reactions during thermal ALE are defined by fluorination followed by ligand exchange using metal precursors. The self-limiting nature of both the fluorination and ligand-exchange reactions enables atomic-level control of the etch depth. Selectivity in thermal ALE can be very high between materials due to the use of targeted chemistry and the absence of energetic ions and radicals. Etch selectivities above 1000:1 have been demonstrated.

## Key Points
- Defines thermal ALE as sequential fluorination + ligand-exchange reactions
- Covers ALE of oxides (Al2O3, HfO2, ZrO2, etc.), nitrides (SiN, TiN), and metals (Ni, Co, W)
- Demonstrates etch selectivity above 1000:1, far exceeding conventional RIE
- Self-limiting reactions enable atomic-level depth control (sub-angstrom precision)
- Authored by Steven George, a pioneer of both ALD and thermal ALE
